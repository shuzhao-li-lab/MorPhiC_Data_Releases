#PROJECT
PR:PROJECT_TITLE                 	Molecular Phenotypes of Null Alleles in Cells
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	The 2020 NHGRI Strategic Vision laid out a set of “bold predictions for human
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	genomics by 2030” including elucidating the biological function(s) of each
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	human gene. The Molecular Phenotypes of Null Alleles in Cells (MorPhiC) seeks to
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	address this element of the strategic vision. Through the comprehensive
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	generation of null alleles for every human gene and then cataloging the
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	resulting molecular and cellular phenotypes, the mechanisms that relate gene
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	function to observed phenotypes can be determined. Furthermore, the resulting
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	catalog of knockouts and phenotypes will be made available for broader use by
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	the biomedical community. Although multiple approaches can be leveraged to
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	measure molecular and cellular phenotypes resulting from gene knockouts,
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	metabolomics and lipidomics (i.e., biochemical phenotyping) provides an avenue
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	to understand the link between gene function and phenotypes at a molecular
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	level. This project consists of studies performed to biochemical phenotype of
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	cell lines and other samples generated as part of MorPhiC. Resources: 1.
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	https://www.nih.gov/news-events/news-releases/nih-initiative-systematically-investigate-establish-function-every-human-gene
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	2.
PR:PROJECT_SUMMARY               	https://www.genome.gov/research-funding/Funded-Programs-Projects/Molecular-Phenotypes-of-Null-Alleles-in-Cells

#STUDY
ST:STUDY_TITLE                   	Comprehensive Untargeted LC-MS Metabolomics Analysis of HMGCS2 Knockout
ST:STUDY_TITLE                   	iPSCs(Supernatant/Media)
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	Human induced pluripotent stem cells (hIPSCs) are a key tool for biomedical
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	research. In this study, human induced pluripotent stem cells of the KOLF2.2J
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	lineage were generated with null allele mutation for HMGCS2. These cell lines
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	were cultured on several types of media with and without various additives and
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	were differentiated into primitive syncytium. Biochemical phenotyping, i.e.,
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	untargeted metabolomics and lipidomics profiling, was performed on both
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	differentiated and undifferentiated cells using a Thermo Scientific Q Exactive
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	HF-X Mass Spectrometer coupled to a Thermo Scientific Transcend LX-2 Duo UHPLC
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	system, equipped with an HES-II ionization source, using both positive and
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	negative ionization modes. This dataset, combined with the other -omics level
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	data generated through MorPhiC on these cell lines, provides a molecular
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	foundation for understanding the implications of specific gene deletions in
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	these cell lines and their derivative cell types (e.g., primitive syncytium).
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	This study includes data generated from the cell pellets of the above cell
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	lines. This study was funded, in part, through UM1HG012651 which established the
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	JAX MorPhiC Center, a MorPhiC Phase 1 Data Production Research and Development
ST:STUDY_SUMMARY                 	Center at the Jackson Laboratory for Genomic Medicine.

TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	(Millipore Sigma, # 57263) were added at day 0 and onwards to induce trophoblast
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	lineage (BS condition). For the induction of primitive syncytium, 1 mM A-83
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	(TOCRIS, #2939) was added at the end of Day 2. Media was refreshed every 2 days
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	until the end of differentiation (Day 6). On day 6, TE media was replaced by the
TR:TREATMENT_SUMMARY             	StemFlex media with or without addition of nutrients 24 hours prior to
